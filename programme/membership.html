Membership

KBI Membership

One community across emerging technologies.

Membership is how we recognise, support, and stay in touch with people and institutions who care about serious emerging-technology work.

Apply to join · Ask a question
Why Join

One ecosystem, many ways in

KBI connects people and institutions across blockchain and trust technologies, data science and AI, and quantum computing. Competitions, training, projects, and — later — certifications all sit in the same ecosystem.

Joining as a member means:

  • You hear about real opportunities early.
  • You are visible to KBI when we need participants, collaborators, and speakers.
  • Your involvement is recorded in one place instead of being scattered across events.

You do not need a perfect CV to start. Curiosity and seriousness are enough.

Individual Membership

For students and professionals

Individual membership is for anyone who wants to be part of the KBI community — from secondary students to senior practitioners.

What you get:

  • Priority access to KBI competitions, workshops, and events.
  • Invitations to training programmes (SkillSprint, ETTI, ABC5, and future offerings).
  • Access to community spaces, study groups, and working / research groups where serious conversations happen.
  • Occasional opportunities to join projects, pilot programmes, or early trials before they are public.
  • A simple record of your involvement that you can reference later — for university applications, career opportunities, or future KBI recognition.

Over time, as the global framework matures, individual membership will also connect to formal credentials and fellowships. For now, its main value is access, visibility, and being part of the loop.

Institutional Membership

For schools, universities, and organisations

Institutions anchor bridges. They host programmes, recognise credentials, and create spaces where emerging-technology work can happen.

If you represent an organisation, membership and partnership can offer:

  • A clear channel into KBI HK’s competitions, training, and events.
  • Early access to collaboration ideas — from guest lectures and project co-supervision to pilot programmes.
  • Visibility to motivated students and practitioners who are already engaged in serious work.
  • Support in designing programmes or initiatives that match your context, not just ours.

We are gradually formalising tiers and labels, but we would rather start with the relationship than with the category.

What Members Can Expect

What membership actually changes

For both individuals and institutions, membership makes a practical difference:

  • You are first in line for limited-capacity opportunities (competitions, circles, clinics, projects).
  • It is easier for us to match you to relevant programmes when we know your interests and context.
  • When we design something new, we usually invite members to test and shape it before it goes public.
  • Over time, your track record with KBI becomes part of your story — not as a badge in isolation, but as a pattern of work.

Membership is not a gate in front of KBI. It is how we know who to work with more deeply.

How to Join

Apply to join

If you are interested in being part of KBI HK — as a student, professional, educator, or organisation — the first step is simply to introduce yourself.

We review each application individually and will follow up with next steps. Membership is free for students and early-stage professionals.
