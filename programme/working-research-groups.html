Community & Research

Working & Research Groups

For those who want to go deeper.

KBI’s working groups and research initiatives bring together students, academics, and practitioners to investigate emerging-technology questions that go beyond any single competition cycle. They are spaces for deeper study, sustained collaboration, and the kind of thinking that cannot always fit into a short programme or event.

What They Are

Communities of inquiry and practice

Some people come to KBI through competitions. Others stay because they want to keep asking harder questions.

Working and research groups create room for that next step. They allow members to explore a domain over time, share knowledge, test ideas, and contribute to conversations that are still evolving across technology, governance, education, and society.

Group Types

Different kinds of groups

Under this umbrella, KBI may run several kinds of group-based initiatives, depending on the topic, available mentors, and level of community interest.

  • Working Groups — short- to medium-term communities focused on practical problem-solving, discussion, and knowledge sharing.
  • Research Groups — longer-term explorations, literature-led study, reproducibility work, or research collaborations with external partners.
  • Industry Research Labs & Projects — group-based initiatives embedded in, or co-developed with, companies and practitioners.
  • Infrastructure & Innovation Initiatives — efforts focused on shared tools, platforms, testbeds, and longer-horizon ecosystem building.
Current Areas

Current and emerging areas

Themes may evolve over time, but current and emerging areas of interest include:

AI Safety & Ethics

Generative AI Tooling

Self-Sovereign Identity

Cybersecurity & Privacy

Quantum Computing

Digital Economics

Digital Publishing

Some groups are discussion-led. Others may produce notes, tools, events, internal briefs, prototype systems, or course and certification ideas. Not every group is formalised to the same degree, and that flexibility is intentional.

Moonshot Ideas

Long-horizon initiatives

Some ideas are too large to sit neatly inside a single working group. These may develop as long-horizon initiatives that combine research, infrastructure thinking, and experimentation across multiple years.

Current examples include No-CAP, Acredemic, and Virtual Königsberg — initiatives that reflect KBI’s interest in identity, governance, education, and technological infrastructure as connected problems rather than isolated ones.

Research Posture

Serious, but still growing

These groups are not meant to imitate established laboratories overnight. They are meant to create serious spaces where emerging talent can think with others, investigate difficult questions, and gradually contribute to meaningful work.

Some groups may remain exploratory. Others may eventually become more formal collaborations, publications, programmes, or technical projects. The structure can evolve; the commitment to rigour remains.
