I²OL — International Individual Olympiad League

The I²OL Scheme

I²OL is the umbrella programme for three subject olympiads run in Hong Kong through KBI HK: blockchain (IBCOL), data science (IDSOL), and quantum computing (IQCOL). Each olympiad tests deep competence in one domain — separately from the cross-disciplinary ETO Eastern Conference (2026).

Understanding I²OL

One scheme, three olympiads

The I²OL scheme groups Hong Kong’s official qualifying rounds for three international technology olympiads under one coherent structure. Students typically choose one olympiad track and build depth in that subject — blockchain, data science, or quantum — through a full competition cycle of preparation, submission, and presentation.

KBI Hong Kong organises and supports IBCOL HK, IDSOL HK, and IQCOL HK locally: registration, workshops, judging, and pathways to global finals. The scheme name appears in navigation so you can find all three olympiads in one place — not because they are a single combined competition.

How it fits together

From I²OL track to ETO

The orientation pathway is intentional: build subject mastery through an I²OL olympiad first; outstanding performers may then be invited or qualified to participate in ETO — which uses its own format and adjudication. ETO is not described as the finals of IBCOL, IDSOL, or IQCOL.

01

Choose your olympiad

Register for IBCOL HK, IDSOL HK, or IQCOL HK — one primary track per cycle unless rules state otherwise.

02

Prepare & compete locally

Workshops, team formation, written submission, and Hong Kong presentation day under I²OL rules.

03

Advance internationally

Top Hong Kong teams represent the city at the global IBCOL, IDSOL, or IQCOL finals for their track.

04

ETO pathway (where applicable)

Qualification or invitation to ETO — including ETO Eastern Conference (9–11 Oct 2026, CDNIS) — follows ETO’s separate criteria.
