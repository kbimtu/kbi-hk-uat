Event Recap & Gallery

2024 Recap & Gallery

2024 was a dense year with local blockchain and data-science finals, school partnerships, crash courses, mentorship sessions, and international delegation activity in Amsterdam. Training, competition, and international representation fit together within one annual cycle.

2023 Gallery ↑ All Events 2025 Gallery
01
Kick-off Session February – April 2024 HKPC, Hong Kong

HKAGE x HKBCOL Kick-off Session

The HKAGE x HKBCOL Kick-off Session introduced HKAGE students and teachers to the Hong Kong Blockchain Olympiad 2024 structure, timeline, and training pathway.

Overview

Held at HKPC, the session helped participants understand how HKBCOL 2024 would unfold, what support structures were available, and how students could prepare meaningfully for the competition journey. It created a shared starting point for both students and educators.

What the session covered

  • The structure and purpose of HKBCOL 2024.
  • Key milestones, timelines, and expectations.
  • The training and preparation pathway for participants.
  • Early orientation for students and teachers joining the cycle.

Why it mattered

A strong competition cycle begins with clarity. This kick-off session helped translate interest into readiness, giving participants a better sense of how to engage seriously with blockchain learning and competition work.



02
Crash Course August 2024 Hong Kong Science and Technology Parks (HKSTP)

IDSOL HK 2024 Crash Course

This concentrated crash course gave IDSOL HK participants rapid technical ramp-up in preparation for the October data science finals.

Overview

The programme focused on acceleration — helping participants consolidate their skills, strengthen technical confidence, and approach the upcoming finals with greater clarity. Its short format reflected the urgency and intensity of final-stage preparation.

What participants gained

  • Focused technical preparation ahead of the finals.
  • A faster ramp-up in relevant data science competencies.
  • Greater confidence entering the October competition stage.

Why it mattered

Crash courses like this are important transitional moments between general learning and competition performance. They help participants move from being interested learners to more prepared finalists.



03
Finals August 2024 Hong Kong Productivity Council

IBCOL HK 2024 Hong Kong Finals

The IBCOL HK 2024 finals brought local teams together to present blockchain projects and determine Hong Kong's representatives for the international stage.

Overview

At the Hong Kong Productivity Council, teams presented their ideas, demonstrated their preparation, and competed for the opportunity to represent Hong Kong internationally. The finals served as both a showcase of student work and a gateway to the next level of competition.

What defined the finals

  • Local teams presenting blockchain-focused projects.
  • Selection of Hong Kong representatives for the international stage.
  • A public-facing moment for student technical effort and teamwork.

Why it mattered

Regional finals are where preparation becomes visible. The event highlighted the seriousness of the Hong Kong pipeline and the growing ability of local teams to contribute to the broader international blockchain competition community.



04
Preparation Session August 2024 Hong Kong Science and Technology Parks (HKSTP)

IBCOL 2024 Socratic Session

The IBCOL 2024 Socratic Session gave teams space to test and refine their thinking through structured questioning and feedback ahead of IBCOL 2024.

Overview

Rather than functioning as a typical workshop, the Socratic Session created a more reflective and demanding environment. Through questioning and guided critique, teams were encouraged to sharpen their logic, clarify assumptions, and strengthen the foundations of their projects.

What teams practiced

  • Responding to structured questioning.
  • Clarifying project logic and assumptions.
  • Refining arguments before higher-stakes presentation.
  • Strengthening analytical depth through feedback.

Why it mattered

The strongest competition work is not only well-made but well-defended. This session helped teams build intellectual resilience and improved their ability to explain, justify, and refine their ideas under scrutiny.



05
Finals October 2024 City University of Hong Kong

IDSOL HK 2024 Hong Kong Finals

The IDSOL HK 2024 finals brought together local teams to present data science and AI solution designs and determine Hong Kong's representatives for the international stage in Amsterdam.

Overview

Hosted at City University of Hong Kong in October 2024, the Hong Kong finals were the culmination of the regional cycle of the International Data Science Olympiad (IDSOL 2024). Teams presented comprehensive data science and artificial intelligence solution designs to address real-world challenges, demonstrating both technical depth and practical thinking.

Shortlisted teams from this round were selected to represent Hong Kong at the IDSOL 2024 international finals in Amsterdam later in the year.

What defined the finals

  • Local teams presenting end-to-end data science and AI solution designs for real-world problems.
  • Emphasis on problem framing, data strategy, model choices, and how solutions would operate in practice.
  • Selection of Hong Kong representatives for the international IDSOL 2024 finals in Amsterdam.

Why it mattered

The Hong Kong finals marked the point where months of preparation and iteration became visible. The event highlighted the strength of the local data and AI talent pipeline and showed that Hong Kong students can engage with complex, applied data science work at an international standard.

For KBI HK's history, IDSOL HK 2024 stands as a key moment in building serious, competition-tested capacity in data science and AI among students in and around Hong Kong.



06
International Delegation November 2024 Amsterdam

ETO 2024 HKSAR Delegation in Amsterdam

A Hong Kong delegation, including THEi participants, travelled to Amsterdam for the Emerging Technologies Olympiad, where they competed and engaged with international peers and judges.

Overview

ETO 2024 brought blockchain and data science together under a broader emerging-technologies frame, and the Hong Kong delegation took part in that international shift. Their participation reflected not only competition readiness, but growing visibility for Hong Kong within the wider KBI-linked ecosystem.

What the delegation experienced

  • International competition and exchange.
  • Engagement with peers and judges from other regions.
  • Participation in a broader emerging-technologies platform, not just a single-discipline event.

Why it mattered

This delegation represented an important outward-facing step for Hong Kong participants. It showed that local students were not only preparing for domestic programmes, but also entering conversations and competitions at an international level.
