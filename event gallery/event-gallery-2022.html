Event Recap & Gallery

2022 Recap & Gallery

In 2022, KBI Hong Kong's ecosystem expanded beyond blockchain alone into broader fintech and Greater Bay Area activity. Through the Fintech Olympiad and GBA International Blockchain Olympiad, students across Hong Kong and the region engaged with real-world problem solving and cross-border participation.

2021 Gallery ↑ All Events 2023 Gallery
01
FinTech Competition May 2022 Online

Fintech Olympiad 2022

Fintech Olympiad 2022 challenged Greater Bay Area university students to propose real-world fintech solutions through an online competition programme hosted by City University of Hong Kong with support from HSBC.

Overview

FTOL 2022 marked an important widening of the KBI-linked competition ecosystem. Rather than focusing only on blockchain as a standalone domain, it invited students to think more broadly about financial technology, innovation, and applied solutions to practical problems.

What participants did

  • Proposed real-world fintech solutions.
  • Took part in a Greater Bay Area university-level challenge.
  • Presented work through an online finals format.
  • Competed across multiple award categories.

Why it mattered

This event signalled an expansion in scope. It showed that the ecosystem around student technical competitions could move from blockchain-specific learning into wider fintech questions, while still keeping a strong emphasis on practical application and regional relevance.

FTOL 2022 helped position Hong Kong students within a broader conversation about finance, technology, and innovation across the Greater Bay Area.



02
Greater Bay Area Competition August 2022 Online / Gather Town

Greater Bay Area International Blockchain Olympiad 2022 (GBA IBCOL 2022)

GBA IBCOL 2022 brought together secondary and tertiary teams from across Mainland China, Hong Kong, and Macao through a virtual platform hosted on Gather Town, addressing real-world problems with blockchain under sector-specific themes.

Overview

The competition extended the blockchain Olympiad model into a more explicitly regional format. By gathering teams from across the Greater Bay Area and beyond, it created a cross-border environment where students could work on applied blockchain problems while engaging with peers from different education systems and contexts.

What defined the event

  • Participation from secondary and tertiary teams.
  • Cross-regional engagement spanning Mainland China, Hong Kong, and Macao.
  • Blockchain problem-solving across sector themes such as health, education, legal, and finance.
  • A fully virtual competition environment using Gather Town.

Why it mattered

GBA IBCOL 2022 demonstrated that the competition ecosystem could grow geographically as well as academically. It linked Hong Kong more directly to regional talent development and showed how blockchain education could be framed around sectoral problem solving rather than technology alone.

The 2022 edition reflected a larger ambition: not only to teach blockchain, but to connect students across borders through shared challenges and emerging-technology thinking.
