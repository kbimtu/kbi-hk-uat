Event Recap & Gallery

2021 Recap & Gallery

In 2021, international programming continued under hybrid and cross-border conditions. KBI Hong Kong remained connected to the wider competition network as major blockchain events were delivered through a mix of in-person and online formats.

2020 Gallery ↑ All Events 2022 Gallery
01
Training Series September – November 2020 Online / Hybrid

HKBCOL 2021 Blockchain Training Series

This free multi-week training series prepared students for the Hong Kong Blockchain Olympiad 2021 through structured teaching across blockchain fundamentals, design, and development tracks.

Overview

The series was designed to turn interest into readiness. Instead of asking students to enter competition with only superficial knowledge, it created a more guided pathway into the core ideas, workflows, and technical perspectives behind blockchain project work.

What participants learned

  • Blockchain fundamentals.
  • Design and development perspectives.
  • Preparation for HKBCOL 2021 and international qualification.

Why it mattered

Training programmes like this made the competition ecosystem more accessible and more serious at the same time. They helped students approach Olympiad participation with stronger foundations and a clearer sense of progression.



02
Regional Competition April 2021 CityUHK / Online

Hong Kong Blockchain Olympiad 2021 (HKBCOL 2021)

City University of Hong Kong co-organised HKBCOL 2021, where teams including Credify and Infi.io earned Bronze, Gold, and Best Prototype awards for blockchain solutions in credentials and project finance.

Overview

HKBCOL 2021 continued the regional competition pathway under mixed delivery conditions. Even with online constraints, the event sustained a serious platform for student teams to present blockchain ideas and gain recognition for their technical and problem-solving work.

What stood out

  • Recognition for teams such as Credify and Infi.io.
  • Award categories including Bronze, Gold, and Best Prototype.
  • Student projects focused on practical blockchain use cases such as credentials and project finance.

Why it mattered

The event showed that meaningful student innovation could still thrive under hybrid conditions. It also reinforced Hong Kong's role as a local stage for blockchain project development and qualification into the wider international ecosystem.



03
International Finals October 2021 Dhaka / Online

International Blockchain Olympiad 2021 (IBCOL 2021)

The IBCOL 2021 grand finale took place as a hybrid event in Dhaka and online, with Hong Kong-linked teams such as EduChain receiving recognition including an Award of Merit for education-focused blockchain solutions.

Overview

IBCOL 2021 brought together teams through a hybrid format that connected in-person hosting with online participation. It maintained the international character of the Olympiad while adapting to the practical realities of the time.

What the finals offered

  • A hybrid international finals format.
  • Recognition for Hong Kong-linked teams including EduChain.
  • Global visibility for student blockchain solutions developed around real use cases.

Why it mattered

This event demonstrated continuity. Even under difficult circumstances, the international competition pathway remained open, and Hong Kong participants could still contribute meaningfully to the global conversation around blockchain applications.
