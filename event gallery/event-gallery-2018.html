Event Recap & Gallery

2017–2018 Recap & Gallery

2017–2018 marked the founding phase of KBI Hong Kong and the arrival of the first International Blockchain Olympiad. It was a period of first principles teaching, institutional beginnings, and the creation of a more structured emerging-technology programme ecosystem in the city.

↑ All Events 2019 Gallery
01
Foundational Course February 2018 CityUHK

Blockchain From First Principles Teaching Course

This foundational short course, Blockchain From First Principles, was taught by Dr. Lawrence Ma and hosted at CityUHK. It introduced students to core blockchain concepts, paradigms, and platforms, while explaining why blockchain matters beyond surface-level hype.

Overview

The course established an educational foundation at a time when blockchain was still often discussed more as a trend than as a serious field of study. Its first-principles approach aimed to give students conceptual clarity before any competition or application work began.

What students explored

  • Core blockchain concepts.
  • Key paradigms and platforms.
  • Why blockchain is useful beyond buzzwords and speculation.

Why it mattered

Strong ecosystems often begin with strong teaching. This course helped create an early intellectual base for the later programmes, competitions, and training structures that followed.



02
First International Finals May 2018 CityUHK with University of Waterloo

International Blockchain Olympiad 2018 (IBCOL 2018)

The International Blockchain Olympiad 2018 was convened by Dr. Lawrence Ma, with finals co-hosted by City University of Hong Kong and the University of Waterloo. Student teams presented blockchain and distributed ledger applications from technical, business, and legal perspectives.

Overview

As the first edition of the International Blockchain Olympiad, the 2018 finals established the core model for what the programme could become. It treated blockchain as a multidisciplinary field and gave student teams a platform to present ideas that crossed technical, commercial, and regulatory dimensions.

What defined the event

  • The inaugural International Blockchain Olympiad.
  • Co-hosting by CityUHK and the University of Waterloo.
  • Student presentations spanning technical, business, and legal viewpoints.
