Event Recap & Gallery

2023 Recap & Gallery

In 2023, KBI-linked activity in Hong Kong centred on local blockchain teaching, student preparation, and the Hong Kong Blockchain Olympiad, alongside the International Blockchain Olympiad finals held in Amsterdam. The year connected classroom exposure, local competition, and international presentation on a single pathway.

2022 Gallery ↑ All Events 2024 Gallery
01
Lecture Series April 2023 HKBU

HKBU Blockchain Lectures 2023

The HKBU Blockchain Lectures 2023 introduced students to core blockchain concepts and real-world applications while connecting that learning to Olympiad-related opportunities.

Overview

Hosted at Hong Kong Baptist University, the lecture series gave students a structured first look at blockchain beyond the level of buzzwords and headlines. It positioned blockchain as a field that could be studied, discussed, and developed through further project work and competition participation.

What students explored

  • Core blockchain concepts and terminology.
  • Real-world applications of distributed ledger technologies.
  • How blockchain learning can connect to Olympiad opportunities and project development.

Why it mattered

This series helped widen access to blockchain education in a university setting. It also showed how KBI-linked programming could connect foundational teaching with more applied and competitive forms of learning.

For many participants, the lectures were not just an introduction to blockchain, but an introduction to a wider ecosystem of emerging-technology engagement.



02
Regional Competition September 2023 Inno Network, HKPC

Hong Kong Blockchain Olympiad 2023 (HKBCOL 2023)

HKBCOL 2023 brought Hong Kong teams together at HKPC's Inno Network to present blockchain solutions, compete for regional recognition, and qualify for the international stage.

Overview

At Inno Network, participating teams presented their ideas in a structured competition setting that combined innovation, technical thinking, and communication. The event provided a platform for local students to test their work publicly and measure it against regional standards.

What defined the event

  • Hong Kong teams presenting blockchain-based solutions.
  • Regional recognition for standout student work.
  • Qualification opportunities connected to the international finals.

Why it mattered

HKBCOL is where local preparation becomes visible. It gave students a serious stage on which to present their thinking and helped strengthen the public presence of blockchain education and innovation in Hong Kong.

The 2023 edition reinforced Hong Kong's role as a place where student blockchain ideas could move from classroom learning into public competition.



03
International Finals November 2023 Hogeschool Inholland, Amsterdam

International Blockchain Olympiad 2023 at DigiQuest

The IBCOL 2023 finals were held as part of DigiQuest 2023 at Hogeschool Inholland, where teams presented blockchain projects in an international, in-person setting.

Overview

As part of DigiQuest 2023 in Amsterdam, the finals created an international space for student teams to present, defend, and share their blockchain projects face to face. It was both a competition and an exchange environment, bringing together technical ideas, public presentation, and cross-border interaction.

What the finals offered

  • An international in-person setting for blockchain project presentation.
  • A platform for teams to engage with global peers and judges.
  • A culminating stage for projects developed through local and regional pathways.

Why it mattered

International finals give students more than recognition; they give perspective. The Amsterdam setting highlighted how local preparation in places like Hong Kong connects to a broader international conversation around blockchain, innovation, and emerging technology education.

IBCOL 2023 showed that student project work could travel well beyond its place of origin and stand within a genuinely international arena.
