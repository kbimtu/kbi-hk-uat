Event Recap & Gallery

2019 Recap & Gallery

In 2019, KBI Hong Kong deepened its local activity through training sessions while helping deliver the second International Blockchain Olympiad in Hong Kong. The year brought together technical preparation, international participation, and a stronger public presence for blockchain education.

2017–2018 Gallery ↑ All Events 2020 Gallery
01
Training Sessions February 2019 CityUHK

IBCOL HK Training Sessions

A series of IBCOL HK training sessions covered both technical and legal perspectives, with modules led by speakers including Heidy Ho and Natalie Lau. The programme helped prepare teams for the 2019 competition cycle with a broader understanding of blockchain work.

Overview

These sessions reflected an increasingly mature training model. Rather than presenting blockchain as only a technical discipline, the programme introduced students to the legal and multidisciplinary thinking required for stronger competition and project design.

What participants gained

  • Exposure to both technical and legal perspectives.
  • Preparation for the 2019 competition cycle.
  • Stronger grounding for project development and presentation.

Why it mattered

Training helped raise the level of participation before teams reached the competition stage. It also showed a more serious educational philosophy: blockchain ideas needed not only coding, but context, reasoning, and broader literacy.



02
International Finals July 2019 CityUHK / HKSTP InnoCentre

International Blockchain Olympiad 2019 (IBCOL 2019)

City University of Hong Kong hosted the 2019 IBCOL finals in partnership with Hong Kong Science and Technology Park. Thirty-four teams gathered in Hong Kong, presenting at CityU and exhibiting at HKSTP InnoCentre before the final pitch and awards ceremony.

Overview

IBCOL 2019 brought a major international blockchain event physically into Hong Kong. With presentations, exhibitions, and final pitches spread across CityUHK and HKSTP InnoCentre, the event combined academic, public, and innovation-facing elements into one visible platform.

What defined the finals

  • Thirty-four shortlisted teams gathering in Hong Kong.
  • Presentations at CityUHK.
  • Exhibitions at HKSTP InnoCentre.
  • Final pitches and awards as the culmination of the international cycle.

Why it mattered

Hosting the finals in Hong Kong strengthened the city's visibility within the international student blockchain ecosystem. It also marked a moment when local educational activity and global competition were clearly meeting on the same stage.
