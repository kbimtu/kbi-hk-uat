Event Recap & Gallery

2020 Recap & Gallery

2020 marked a major shift in format as programmes moved online and into hybrid delivery during the pandemic. It also marked the debut of the International Data Science Olympiad, signalling a broader expansion of KBI Hong Kong's competition and training ecosystem.

2019 Gallery ↑ All Events 2021 Gallery
01
Professional Training September 2019 – April 2020 CityUHK / Online

Certified Blockchain Professional Training Course

This multi-month Certified Blockchain Professional course, delivered in partnership with City University of Hong Kong, combined on-campus and online teaching to deepen participants' understanding of blockchain technology and its applications.

Overview

The course bridged two eras at once: traditional in-person teaching and rapid online adaptation. Across its duration, it gave participants the opportunity to engage blockchain in a more structured, professional, and application-oriented way.

What the course offered

  • Extended training over multiple months.
  • A partnership format involving CityUHK.
  • Deeper study of blockchain technology and real-world applications.
  • A blended teaching model spanning on-campus and online delivery.

Why it mattered

This course showed that rigorous learning could continue even as formats changed. It also reflected KBI HK's ability to sustain depth, not just one-off events, during a highly uncertain year.



02
Regional Finals June 2020 Online

HKBCOL 2020 Hong Kong Regional Finals

The Hong Kong regional finals for the Blockchain Olympiad moved fully online in 2020, with local teams presenting blockchain solutions remotely while competing for places at the international finals.

Overview

Transitioning the regional finals online required more than a format change; it required maintaining credibility, seriousness, and participant momentum under new conditions. The event ensured that local teams could still present, compete, and progress.

What defined the event

  • Fully online regional presentations.
  • Continued qualification opportunities for the international finals.
  • Sustained participation from Hong Kong teams during disruption.

Why it mattered

HKBCOL 2020 was a proof of continuity. It showed that student project work and regional recognition could carry on even when physical events were no longer possible.



03
International Finals July 2020 Online

International Blockchain Olympiad 2020 (IBCOL 2020)

The 2020 International Blockchain Olympiad finals were held online, preserving an international platform for student blockchain projects despite global travel and gathering restrictions.

Overview

IBCOL 2020 adapted the international finals to a fully online environment while keeping the core purpose intact: giving student teams a serious global stage on which to present their ideas. It demonstrated resilience at the level of both organisers and participants.

What the event represented

  • A fully online international finals format.
  • Continued global visibility for student blockchain teams.
  • A strong commitment to keeping the competition pathway active despite restrictions.

Why it mattered

The online finals protected international continuity. For Hong Kong and other participating regions, it ensured that local effort still connected to a wider world rather than ending at the border of disruption.
