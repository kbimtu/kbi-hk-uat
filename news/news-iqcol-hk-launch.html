Introducing IQCOL:
A New Quantum Olympiad

A new pathway for students serious about quantum.

KBI HK Competitions Team
KBI Hong Kong
1 May 2026
5 min read

There is a new member of the I²OL olympiad family: IQCOL — the quantum computing and information olympiad.

Alongside IBCOL (blockchain) and IDSOL (data science), IQCOL offers a structured way for students to engage with quantum concepts, from foundational ideas to more advanced problem-solving, in a competition format that still prioritises understanding over memorisation.

Why IQCOL matters

Quantum computing is often treated as distant or purely theoretical. IQCOL is designed to:

  • make serious quantum concepts accessible to motivated students
  • provide a clear progression from basic literacy to deeper engagement
  • connect participants to a community of peers, mentors, and future opportunities

Participants may encounter topics such as qubits and measurement, quantum gates and simple circuits, quantum error and noise, and implications for cryptography and security.

How IQCOL fits into I²OL

IQCOL joins:

  • IBCOL – International Blockchain Olympiad
  • IDSOL – Data Science Olympiad

Together, the three olympiads cover blockchain and trust technologies, data and AI, and quantum computing and information technologies. Students may choose to focus on one olympiad or participate across multiple, depending on their interests and preparation.

Format and expectations

IQCOL runs as an international olympiad with published rules, timelines, and problem formats.

Students can expect:

  • written and problem-solving components
  • an emphasis on clarity of reasoning rather than rote memorisation
  • problems that reward careful thought and conceptual understanding

Full details for each competition cycle are available on the official IQCOL website.

Who should consider IQCOL

IQCOL is suitable for:

  • secondary and university students with strong interest in mathematics, physics, or computer science
  • participants from quantum seminars, reading groups, or related activities
  • newcomers who are ready to work seriously and are not afraid of unfamiliar concepts

Prior quantum programming experience is not required; a willingness to learn and think carefully matters more.

Next steps

If you are interested in IQCOL and quantum more broadly:

  • Join KBI HK membership to receive updates on relevant events and support opportunities
  • Visit the I²OL Scheme page to see how IQCOL relates to the other olympiads
  • Explore upcoming quantum-themed events and study groups to start building your foundations
